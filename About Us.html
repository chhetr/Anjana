Our Story

As women of colors, before coming to the United States, we never thought of our skin colors, never thought of being a person of color, but once we came here, our skin colors started to matter, and it became part of our identity, part of our lives and part of our daily routine. Our race was never a part of us; we identified ourselves as part of the country we were born in or found refuge. But even though racism is not transparent in our state of origin, racism is, and it is in a different way that is hard to realize. We agreed with Mills as racism is the core of social contact because racism is experienced differently in other parts of the world and here in the United-States racism is more transparent and more aware due to skin color that became part of people identity and the state of one skin color is better than the other. But in other places, racism is experienced differently, for example, through politics and more.
