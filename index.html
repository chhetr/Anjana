What does it mean to be a member of society?

To be a member of society means to realize how you view yourself vs. how society views you
How you view yourself: culture, education, race, background, religion
How does society view you: social class, sexual identity, ethnicity, race

Being a member of society is being part of something greater than ourselves. Everyone wants belonging.
Society is the ultimate qualifier for being part of something or someplace where you might, at times, sacrifice certain rights or liberties to belong or contribute to the harmonious co-existence of that community or society.
Joining political parties, specific charitable organizations, and clubs is how people in the society want to belong in something more significant. Being a productive member of the community means giving up individual freedoms to guarantee our protection and protecting people we care about the most and things we care about.
Being a productive member of society means being an asset rather than a liability to those around us.
